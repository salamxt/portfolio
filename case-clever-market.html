The Challenge

A Country That Didn't Trust Online Shopping

In 2018, Kazakhstan's e-commerce was essentially non-existent. Only 23% of the population had ever made an online purchase.

Horror stories circulated about orders that never arrived, payments that disappeared, products that looked nothing like the photos. Cash was king, and handing over money before seeing the goods felt like gambling.

ğŸ’¡ Key Insight

We weren't competing with other e-commerce platforms. We were competing with the local bazaar â€” where you could touch products, negotiate prices, and walk away if something felt wrong.

The Approach

Design for Skeptics, Not Early Adopters

Most e-commerce platforms are designed for people who already trust online shopping. We had to design for people who actively distrusted it.

1. Cash on Delivery as the Default

We made cash on delivery the primary payment option â€” not hidden in settings, but prominently featured. This single decision removed the biggest barrier.

2. Real-Time Order Tracking

Anxiety peaks after payment. We built GPS tracking that showed exactly where your order was, with accurate ETAs.

3. Visual Trust Signals

Every seller had verified badges, customer reviews with photos, and response time indicators. We made the invisible visible.
